Foo Fighters

A Quick Guide to Allison Marshall's Favorite Band

Description

Foo Fighters is a rock band that was formed in 1994 and released its first studio album in 1995. For more information on the band, its history, and current tours please see the Foo Fighters Home Page.

Band Members

The Foo Fighters has occassionally changed band members over its twenty-five year timeline, but the individuals listed below are long-term members who still perform with the band.

  • Dave Grohl - Vocals and Guitar
  • Nate Mendel - Bass
  • Pat Smear - Guitar and Backup Vocals
  • Taylor Hawkins - Drums and Backup Vocals
  • Chris Shiflett - Guitar and Backup Vocals
  • Rami Jaffee - Keyboard and Piano

Favorite Albums

Foo Fighters has released nine albums, four EPs, and thirty six singles in total. Below, I identify and review my favorite three of these studio albums in order.

There is Nothing Left to Lose
This album released at the end of 1999, and it is frequently ranked as one of the most popular Foo Fighter's albums of all time. I was only a child when this album was released, but my older brother would blast this album on repeat throughout the house. Three songs from this album-- "Learn to Fly", "Aurora", and "Headwires"-- rank amongst my favorite songs of all time. This album is perfect for long drives with its great melodies, deep lyrics, and a strong soft rock feel.
The Colour and the Shape
I rank this album as my second favorite album from the Foo Fighters primarily due to the inclusion of the song, "Everlong", which is both my favorite Foo Fighters song as well as my favorite song of all time. This was the first Foo Fighters song I heard as a child due to my older brother's growing love for the band. The album varies from a softer grunge rock to a heavier hard rock, so it speaks to individuals across genres. Following the release of the Foo Fighter's first album in 1995, Dave Grohl was instructed to make his lyrics more meaningful in his Sophomore album. This is reflected throughout The Colour and Shape, where Dave Grohl sings about life, freedom, and a failing marriage.
In Your Honor
Ranking third amongst my favorite all time Foo Fighter's albums is In Your Honor. This album released in 2005 while I was in middle school. The songs in this album have a much stronger rock feel, with heavier guitar, louder lyrics, and a different overall "vibe". A prime example of this comes from the song, "Best of You", which sits as song three on the album. As a teen, the louder style played towards my angst, but as an adult, I can appreciate the meaningful lyrics and differing musical styles throughout
